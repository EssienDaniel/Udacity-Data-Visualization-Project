Exploratory Analysis of Flights Data¶

by Essien Daniel¶

Investigation Overview¶

The Flights dataset report flights in the United States, including carriers, arrival and departure delays, and reasons for delays. The analyses will be trying to answer the following questions:

  • Are some locations/destinations more susceptiple to delays of flights than others?
  • Which are the best carriers(airlines) in terms of minimal flight delays?
  • How does distance contributed to delays? ## Dataset Overview

The dataset considered is year 2008. It consist of 2389217 records and 29 features. Here are the definitions of the features:

The 2008 flight dataset originally had 29 variables and 2389217 records but after removing records that do not show delays(negative values of departure and arrival delays) and also selecting specific variables that will help support the investigation, the dataset now have 870780 records and 13 variables. Observably, taking away negative arrival and departure delays values takes out CancellationCode, Cancelled,

What are the main features of interest in your dataset?¶

The main features considered are the ones that relate to delays, time, location and carriers.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your features of interest?¶

The following features were selected: DepDelay, ArrDelay, State,Airport, Dest, Distance, Cancelled, UniqueCarrier, Month, DayofMonth, CancellationCode, CarrierDelay, WeatherDelay, NASDelay, SecurityDelay
